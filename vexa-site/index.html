Why long-running work needs a new surface

Autonomy creates
an awareness gap.

When a mission outlives the interaction that started it, a dashboard becomes somewhere you must remember to check. Vexa makes state ambient while preserving human authority.

01

Peripheral awareness

Continuous motion says “still working” without demanding focus. Scheduled state adds the useful part: when work resumes.

02

Calibrated trust

Visible success, failure, and source boundaries help users understand capability without implying that the agent is infallible.

03

Human attention routing

Approval posture asks for attention, but the click only opens VEX. The consequential decision never moves into the companion.

04

Outcome integrity

Profit animation is earned only by a positive corrected-ledger result. No projected balances, reconstructed returns, or celebratory guessing.

Design grounding

Apple HIG Lengthy operations need visible, accurate status in a consistent place. NIST AI RMF Human oversight, monitoring, reliability, and accountability must remain explicit. Google PAIR Trust should be calibrated through clear capabilities, limits, and data sources.

Architecture by subtraction

Close enough to know.
Separate enough to trust.

Vexa is an independent Electron process. It observes a deliberately narrow local projection of VEX state and owns no execution capability.
